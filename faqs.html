Frequently Asked Questions

Why are these policies important?

The Student Policy Agreement is a formal document that outlines the expectations and guidelines for all students at the College of Information Technology, Cebu Eastern College, Inc.

These policies are in place to ensure a safe, respectful, and productive learning environment for all students, faculty, and staff. They cover critical areas including:

  • Proper conduct and respect for others
  • Safe and responsible use of laboratory equipment
  • Academic integrity and honesty
  • Data management and personal responsibility
  • Attendance and punctuality
  • Sanctions for violations and consequences
  • Liability and financial responsibility
  • Technology ethics and cybersecurity

Important: By signing this agreement, you acknowledge that you understand these policies and agree to follow them throughout your time at CEC.

Record Keeping & Policy Enforcement

Your submission is permanently recorded and kept for compliance purposes.

When you submit this Policy Agreement Form, your submission is electronically recorded and securely stored in the college's database. This record serves important purposes:

  • Evidence of Acknowledgment: Your signature confirms you have read and understood all policies
  • Enforcement: If you violate any of these policies, your agreement can be referenced in disciplinary proceedings
  • Documentation: The record is maintained in your student file for the duration of your enrollment
  • Future Reference: In case of disputes or violations, the college can verify when you acknowledged these policies
  • Compliance: The record helps CEC maintain institutional compliance and accountability standards
Note: Policy violations may result in disciplinary action, up to and including suspension or expulsion, as documented in the Policy Agreement.

Common Questions & Answers

The Policy Agreement is mandatory for all students. If you do not agree to the terms, you will not be able to access the Computer Laboratory or participate in IT-related classes. We encourage you to review all sections carefully and discuss any concerns with your instructor or the Department Coordinator.

Once you submit the Policy Agreement Form, it becomes a binding agreement for the academic year. You cannot cancel or modify it. However, if policies change significantly, you may be asked to acknowledge an updated version. For questions about your submission, contact the College of Information Technology office.

Sanctions depend on the severity and repetition of violations:

  • Verbal Warning: For first-time minor offenses
  • Written Warning: For repeated or moderate violations
  • Behavioral Contract: For recurring violations
  • Probation Status: For serious or continuous misconduct
  • Suspension Recommendation: For major or critical offenses
  • Permanent Record Entry: Major offenses documented in your official record

Students are financially responsible for any damage caused intentionally or through negligence. Compensation must be made within 30 days of notice. Failure to settle may result in grade withholding, clearance delays, or administrative action. For group activities, responsibility is assessed based on individual involvement.

The college is not liable for any loss of student files due to improper saving. You are responsible for saving your work properly using approved platforms such as Google Drive or GitHub. Always maintain backups of your important work.

Excessive tardiness may affect your class participation and performance grades. Class entry policies are determined by your instructor. It's best to arrive on time and adhere to the stated class schedule.

Yes, absolutely. Academic honesty is mandatory. Cheating, plagiarism, and unauthorized assistance are strictly prohibited. Violations will be treated as serious offenses and may result in failing the course, suspension, or expulsion.

Phones and electronic gadgets may only be used for class-related activities with instructor permission. Personal use, social media, or games are not allowed during laboratory sessions. Keep your device in silent mode to avoid disruptions.

If you witness a policy violation or have concerns about student conduct, report it to:

  • Your instructor
  • Laboratory Custodian
  • Program Coordinator
  • Dean of College of Information Technology
  • Guidance Office

The agreement itself is not on your transcript. However, any sanctions or disciplinary actions resulting from policy violations may be reflected in your academic record. This is why compliance with all policies is important for your academic standing.

The "No Uniform, No ID, No Entry" policy ensures:

  • Security: Verifying authorized students in the laboratory
  • Professional conduct: Maintaining institutional standards
  • Accountability: Tracking attendance and participation
  • Identity verification: Protecting equipment and property

Without proper identification, entry to the laboratory will be denied. Students must comply on their first attempt.

All students must maintain proper appearance standards:

  • Boys: Proper haircut (professional, well-groomed, above collar)
  • Girls: Must wear school tie with uniform
  • All students: Complete school uniform as required

Violations of dress code will prevent laboratory access until compliance. This policy maintains professional standards consistent with college regulations.

Charging personal devices in the laboratory is not authorized. This policy prevents:

  • Electrical hazards and potential fires
  • Distraction from lab work
  • Overloading electrical systems
  • Damage to school equipment

Devices found charging will be confiscated. Repeated violations may result in behavioral contracts and restrictions on lab access.

Sleeping in the computer laboratory is not permitted as it indicates:

  • Lack of engagement in coursework
  • Possible health or attendance concerns
  • Irresponsibility during laboratory hours

Progressive discipline: First offense results in a verbal warning and referral to the clinic for wellness check. Repeated occurrences escalate to written warnings and behavioral contracts. If sleep issues persist, speak with the Student Health Services or your academic advisor.

All violations follow progressive discipline:

  1. Behavioral Contract: Formal agreement with restrictions at guidance office.
  2. Probation Status: Serious misconduct requiring administrative approval
  3. Suspension: Temporary removal from institution
  4. Expulsion: Permanent removal (critical or repeated violations)

Visit the Violations page to see detailed consequences by severity level.
