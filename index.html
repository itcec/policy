Student Policy Agreement Form

I. GENERAL CONDUCT POLICY

  1. Students must maintain proper behavior at all times within the premises of the Computer Laboratory and IT classrooms.
  2. Enter and exit quietly and orderly. Disruptive behavior, shouting, running, or horseplay is strictly prohibited.
  3. Follow the instructions of the Instructor, Lab Custodian, or any authorized personnel promptly.
  4. Respect fellow students, faculty, laboratory equipment, and school property at all times.

II. COMPUTER LABORATORY RULES

A. Equipment Use
  1. Handle computers, peripherals, furniture, and cables with care.
  2. Switching computers ON/OFF is allowed only when instructed by the teacher.
  3. Plugging/unplugging devices is prohibited unless given permission.
  4. Any malfunction or damage must be reported immediately.
B. System & Software Restrictions
  1. Access only teacher-approved websites and platforms.
  2. Downloading, installing, or uninstalling software is strictly prohibited.
  3. Accessing social media, games, or inappropriate content is not allowed.
  4. Inserting flash drives or external USB storage is prohibited unless approved for academic use.
  5. Students must not modify, delete, or tamper with system files.
C. Cleanliness & Safety
  1. No food or drinks inside the computer laboratory.
  2. Maintain cleanliness in the workspace by returning chairs to their proper places, arranging keyboards appropriately, and disposing of all trash before leaving.
  3. Do not touch internal PC components, power outlets, or wiring.

III. CLASSROOM POLICIES

  1. Students must bring necessary materials: notebooks, pens, required devices.
  2. Active participation is encouraged; respect others when speaking.
  3. Academic honesty is mandatory—cheating, plagiarism, and unauthorized assistance are strictly prohibited.
  4. Phones or electronic gadgets may only be used for class-related activities.

IV. DATA MANAGEMENT POLICY

  1. The school is not liable for any loss of student files due to improper saving.
  2. Students must not access, delete, or alter another student's files.

V. ATTENDANCE, TARDINESS & EXAMS

  1. Attendance contributes to grades as indicated in the syllabus or grading system.
  2. Excessive tardiness may affect class participation and performance.
  3. Exam rules must be followed strictly; violation may result in invalidation of the exam.

VI. SANCTIONS FOR VIOLATIONS

Depending on severity and repetition, the following sanctions may apply:

  1. Verbal Warning – for first-time minor offenses.
  2. Written Warning – for repeated or moderate violations; student and instructor must sign the incident report.
  3. Behavioral Contract – for recurring violations; handled by the Program Coordinator.
  4. Probation Status – for serious or continuous misconduct; approved by the Dean.
  5. Suspension Recommendation – for major or critical offenses; forwarded to Guidance and higher administration.
  6. Permanent Record Entry – validated major offenses will be documented in the student's official record.

VII. LIABILITY FOR LABORATORY DAMAGES

  1. Students are financially responsible for any damage caused intentionally or due to negligence.
  2. Compensation must be made within 30 days of notice, unless another arrangement is approved by the department.
  3. Costs may include:
    • Replacement of damaged parts
    • Repair fees
    • Service charges
  4. Group work activities that result in damage will be assessed based on responsibility and involvement.
  5. Failure to settle compensation may result in grade withholding, clearance delay, or administrative action.

VIII. TECHNOLOGY ETHICS & CYBER POLICY

  1. Unauthorized access, hacking attempts, or manipulation of school systems is strictly prohibited.
  2. Violations may lead to disciplinary action and legal referral if necessary.

IX. AGREEMENT & ACKNOWLEDGEMENT

By signing below, I hereby certify that:

  • I have read, understood, and agree to follow all Computer Laboratory Rules, Classroom Policies, Sanctions, and Liability Clauses set by the Cebu Eastern College – College of Information Technology.
  • I understand that failure to follow these rules may result in disciplinary actions, sanctions, or financial liability.
  • I voluntarily agree to abide by all clauses stated in this Policy Agreement.
