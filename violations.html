Policy Violations & Enforcement

Understanding Policy Violations

Policy violations are categorized by severity level. Each violation can result in disciplinary actions ranging from verbal warnings to suspension or expulsion.

All students are expected to comply with CEC policies. Violations will be documented in your student record and may affect your academic standing.

CRITICAL VIOLATIONS

Immediate disciplinary action required - Risk of suspension/expulsion

Unauthorized Access / Hacking Attempts

Description: Unauthorized access to school systems, hacking attempts, or manipulation of computer systems.

Consequences:

  • Immediate referral to Dean and higher administration
  • Potential legal action and law enforcement involvement
  • Possible suspension or expulsion
  • Permanent record documentation
Academic Dishonesty

Description: Cheating, plagiarism, unauthorized assistance in exams or assignments.

Consequences:

  • Automatic failure of the course
  • Referral to Guidance Office
  • Possible suspension for repeated violations
  • Expulsion for serious or multiple offenses
Deliberate Equipment Damage

Description: Intentional damage to laboratory equipment or school property.

Consequences:

  • Full financial responsibility for replacement/repair
  • Grade withholding until compensation is made
  • Clearance delays and administrative action
  • Referral to disciplinary board

MAJOR VIOLATIONS

Serious misconduct - Documented and escalated

No Uniform, No ID, No Entry

Description: Entering the computer laboratory without proper uniform and valid student ID.

Consequences:

  • Entry denial to computer laboratory
  • Behavioral contract at Guidance Office
Improper Dress Code Violation

Description: Boys without proper haircut, girls without ties or improper uniform.

Consequences:

  • Lab access denied until compliance
  • Repeated violations: Probation status consideration
Unauthorized Phone Charging

Description: Charging personal devices in the computer laboratory without authorization.

Consequences:

  • Device confiscation during class and returned at day's end
  • Behavioral contract with restrictions
Sleeping in Computer Laboratory

Description: Sleeping or being unresponsive during laboratory sessions.

Consequences:

  • Behavioral contract
  • Repeated violations: Probation status review
Disruptive or Disrespectful Behavior

Description: Shouting, running, disrespect to faculty or staff, inappropriate language.

Consequences:

  • Behavioral contract
  • Serious offense: Immediate referral to Guidance Office
Unauthorized Software Installation

Description: Installing, downloading, or tampering with software or system files.

Consequences:

  • Lab access restricted pending investigation
  • Probation status consideration

MODERATE VIOLATIONS

Regular infractions - Progressive discipline

Food or Drinks in Laboratory

Description: Consuming food or beverages inside the computer laboratory.

Consequences:

  • ID Confiscation
  • Repeated violations: Sent to Guidance Office
Excessive Tardiness

Description: Repeated late arrivals or absences to laboratory sessions without excuse.

Consequences:

  • Affects class participation grades
  • Entry may be denied per instructor discretion

  • Documented in attendance record
Failure to Maintain Workspace Cleanliness

Description: Not cleaning workspace before leaving, leaving trash or cables untidy.

Consequences:

  • ID Confiscation and Required cleanup
  • Clean the Entire Classroom Laboratory at day's end.
  • Repeated violations: Behavioral contract
Unauthorized File Access

Description: Accessing, deleting, or altering another student's files.

Consequences:

  • Sent to Guidance Office + restoration required
  • Behavioral contract
  • Repeated violations: Probation status consideration
Misuse of Personal Devices

Description: Using phone for games, social media, or personal use during class.

Consequences:

  • ID Confiscation
  • Device confiscation
  • Sent to Guidance Office
Disciplinary Actions Summary
Progressive Discipline Process:
  1. Sent to Guidance Office: Formal counseling, handled by Guidance Counselor.
  2. Behavioral Contract: Formal agreement for behavioral modification, handled by Guidance Counselor.
  3. Probation Status: Serious misconduct, approved by Dean and Guidance Counselor, restrictions on lab access
  4. Suspension Recommendation: Major offenses, forwarded to Guidance and Administration
  5. Expulsion: Critical violations or repeated serious offenses
Important Notes:
  • All violations are documented in student records, forwarded to the Guidance Office
  • Severity determines the starting point in progressive discipline
  • Minor violations may skip steps in progressive discipline
  • Critical violations result in immediate referral to higher authorities
  • Financial liability for damages applies regardless of disciplinary action
